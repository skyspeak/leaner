# Consonant Blends for 4-Year-Olds

## Beginning Consonant Blends (Two Letters)

### S Blends
- **ST** - stop, star, step, stem, stir, stun
- **SP** - spot, spin, spat, sped, span, spit
- **SL** - slap, slip, slop, slid, slam, slot
- **SN** - snap, snip, snob, snug
- **SW** - swat, swim, swig, swum
- **SC** - scab, scan, scat, scud
- **SK** - skip, skid, skim, skin

### L Blends
- **PL** - plan, plug, plop, plum, plus, plot
- **BL** - blob, bled, blip, blot, blue
- **CL** - clap, clip, club, clog, clot
- **FL** - flap, flip, flop, fled, flat, flux
- **GL** - glad, glop, glum, glib, glow, glue
- **SL** - (see S blends above)

### R Blends
- **BR** - brim, brag, bred, brad, bran, brat
- **CR** - crab, cram, crop, crib, crew, crux
- **DR** - drop, drag, drum, draw, drew, drip
- **FR** - frap, fret, frog, from, frug, frit
- **GR** - grab, grid, grin, grow, grub, grit
- **PR** - prep, prim, prop, pram, prod
- **TR** - trap, trip, tree, tray, true, trot

### Other Common Blends
- **TW** - twin, twig, twit
- **DW** - dwam (less common)
- **SM** - smog, smut, smug
- **SP** - (see S blends above)

- **ND** - band, hand, land, sand, wind
- **NK** - bank, sink, tank, wink, pink
- **MP** - camp, jump, lamp, stamp, bump
- **LT** - salt, belt, melt, felt
- **LD** - cold, hold, gold, told, bold
- **LP** - help, yelp, gulp
- **LF** - self, shelf, golf
- **LK** - milk, silk, walk, talk
- **FT** - left, gift, lift, soft
- **PT** - kept, wept, slept, swept
- **CT** - act, fact, pact, tact
- **SK** - mask, desk, risk, task
- **SP** - wasp, clasp, grasp, crisp

## Three-Letter Blends (Advanced for 4-year-olds)

### S Blends
- **SCR** - scrap, scrub, scrim
- **SPR** - spray, sprig, spry
- **STR** - strap, strip, strong
- **SQU** - squab, squad, squat

### Other Three-Letter Blends
- **SPL** - splash, split, splat
- **THR** - three, throw, through
- **SHR** - shred, shrub, shrug

## Recommended Teaching Order for 4-Year-Olds

### Phase 1: Easiest to Recognize and Pronounce
1. **ST** - Very common, easy to hear
2. **PL** - Clear sound, many simple words
3. **BL** - Simple, common words
4. **CL** - Easy to pronounce
5. **FL** - Fun words, easy sounds

### Phase 2: Common and Useful
6. **BR** - Many familiar words
7. **CR** - Common in daily language
8. **DR** - Easy to blend
9. **TR** - Very common blend
10. **GR** - Good for expanding vocabulary

### Phase 3: Expanding Skills
11. **FR** - Slightly more complex
12. **PR** - More challenging
13. **SP** - Common but needs practice
14. **SL** - Similar to other L blends
15. **GL** - Less common but useful

### Phase 4: Advanced for 4-Year-Olds
16. **SN** - Can be tricky
17. **SW** - Requires practice
18. **SC/SK** - Similar sounds
19. **SM** - Less common
20. **TW** - Less frequent

## Notes for Teaching

- **Start with 4-letter words** - Easier to decode
- **Use visual flashcards** - Help with recognition
- **Practice one blend at a time** - Don't overwhelm
- **Use stories and context** - Makes learning fun
- **Focus on common words** - Words they'll encounter daily
- **Ending blends** can be introduced after beginning blends are mastered
- **Three-letter blends** are typically for older children (5-6 years)
